New here? What these terms mean
Today's change — how much the price moved since yesterday's close. Green is up, red is down.
Your holding — enter how many shares you own and the average price you paid; it shows what they're worth now and whether you're ahead.
Open / Prev close — the first price today, and yesterday's final price.
Day high / low — the highest and lowest the price reached today.
52-week range — the lowest and highest price over the past year. Shows where today sits in the bigger picture.
Market cap — the company's total value (share price × all shares). A rough measure of size.
P/E ratio — price compared to profits. A high number means investors are paying a lot for each dollar of earnings, usually signalling high expectations.
Beta — how jumpy a stock is versus the market. Above 1 means it tends to swing more than average.
Analyst view — how professional analysts currently rate the stock: buy, hold, or sell.
Trend projection — the dashed line is a simple straight-line extension of today's movement. It's an illustration, not a prediction.
Chart timeframes — "Live" is the price the app collects itself today (with the projection). 1D/5D/1M/6M/1Y pull real historical data so you can see the bigger picture. A brand-new stock like SPCX will have little history until time passes.
Price alerts — get flagged when a stock crosses a price you set, or makes a big daily move. The % presets follow market norms: most days a stock moves 1–2% (normal), ~5% is considered a significant day, and ~10% is a major one. Alerts only fire while this page is open.
Not financial advice. Prices come from a free public feed and may be delayed up to ~15 minutes — they are not exchange-grade real-time data. The dashed projection is a naive extrapolation of the recent trend for illustration only; it is not a forecast and should not drive decisions. Auto-refresh runs only while this page is open and in front; reopen it to catch up. Data is stored on this device's browser.
